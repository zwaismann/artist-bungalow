01 // The Identity

Brand Essence

Classic Hollywood glamour meets mid-century modernism meets California warmth.

An intimate, art-filled retreat perched in the Hollywood Hills, steps from the legendary Hollywood Bowl. Sophisticated but fun, bold but sun-drenched, editorial but personal.

Design Direction: Stahl House clean lines + Chateau Marmont storied warmth + Eames playful geometry + Palm Springs golden hour. Never sterile, never corporate. The visual language of a well-traveled creative who knows good design but doesn't take themselves too seriously.

Usage Rules

  • Brass is the hero accent, use for all CTAs, section labels, and star ratings.
  • Onyx + Ivory is the primary contrast pair. Avoid pure white (#FFF) or pure black (#000).
  • Terracotta, Olive, and Ocean are supporting accents, not competing leads.
  • Dark sections should use the Onyx background with Ivory and Brass typography.

05 // The Vibe

Voice & Photography

Tone of Voice

  • Warm & Personal: Never sound corporate. Speak with local expertise and casual authority.
  • Dual Perspective: Use the property as the protagonist ("A retreat perched in the hills...") but switch to second-person for experiential moments ("Your mornings start with canyon light...").
  • Sensory Language: Focus on light, breeze, views, and warmth.
  • Confidentiality: Keep hosts private. No host names or personal identities.
  • Formatting: Short sentences. Confident pacing. Never use em-dashes (—).

Visual Layout

  • Asymmetric Grids: Avoid perfectly uniform boxes to keep the layout feeling organic and editorial.
  • Whitespace: Use generous negative space. Let the content breathe.
  • Sharp Edges: No rounded corners on photographs. Keeps the design architectural and crisp.
  • Minimal Decor: Use thin horizontal rules as dividers. Avoid heavy drop shadows or bordered cards.
  • Micro-Interactions: Subtle fade-up scroll reveals (0.7s) and parallax hero images. Hover gallery zooms limited to a gentle 1.05 scale.
